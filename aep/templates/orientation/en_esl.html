ESL

English as a Second language (ESL)

Our ESL Classes are designed to help you increase your Reading, Writing, and Speaking skills in English.

The classes are usually eight weeks long and are taught entirely in English. The teacher will speak English 100% of the time.

The classes you can take will depend on the level of English you currently have and how you test on our placement exam-- accommodations for testing & classes are available for students.


We have classes from Level 1 (No English Ability) to Level 6 (High Fluency in English).

Once you are registered with our program, you never have to re-register again!

Even if you need to take a break for awhile, you do not need to re-register. You can just talk to the Main Office or make a coaching appointment to get back in.


Difference Between DCCAEP ESL & For-Credit ESL


DCCAEP English as a Second Language (ESL) classes are…

  • Free
  • Offered in the morning and evening and meet twice a week
  • Meant to help you socially, personally, and professionally (but don’t give you school credit)
  • Repeatable! You can take classes until you feel ready to move on.

Delgado Community College also offers college credit ESL classes for students studying for a degree or certificate. Some differences are...

  • You pay for college classes
  • You get college credit
  • You earn a grade

None of these things are true for DCCAEP classes.


Modes of Instruction

On campus classes-- We use textbooks, websites, reading, writing and conversation to practice together in a classroom.

All materials are provided, but no children or guests are allowed.


Online classes--We use videos, slides, questions, live video classes to practice. In addition, you may book appointments with your teacher when you need help.

You do need a device and computer skills to study in online classes.


Both types of classes (on campus and online) cover topics like…

  • Personal Information
  • Hobbies
  • School
  • Work
  • Health & Medicine
  • Holidays & Traditions
  • Daily Living
